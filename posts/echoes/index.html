Echoes of Defiance

Salma Tawfik

2024/03/17

In the cavernous expanse of the corporate jungle, I tread lightly, an unwelcome intruder. Each step I take feels like tiptoeing through a minefield of expectations, where the slightest misstep could detonate the fragile facade of acceptance I’ve constructed.

My voice, a symphony of unheard melodies, echoes through the sterile corridors, drowned out by the thunderous applause for those who fit the mold. They speak in tongues of authority, their language foreign to my ears, leaving me stranded on the outskirts of comprehension.

Like a lone dandelion in a field of roses, I stand tall amidst a sea of conformity, my petals wilting under the scorching gaze of their disapproval. They pluck at my identity, tearing away pieces of my soul with each spoken word, until all that’s left is a hollow shell of who I used to be.

I am but a ghost in this realm of the living, haunting the halls of mediocrity with my unseen presence. My ideas, wild and untamed, are shackled by the chains of their skepticism, condemned to wander the labyrinth of rejection without ever finding solace in acceptance.

And yet, I refuse to fade into obscurity, to let their indifference extinguish the flame of my passion. For I am more than just a cog in their machine, more than just a footnote in their narrative of success.

I am the whisper of rebellion in a world of conformity, the echo of defiance in a chorus of compliance.

Char O’Brien // Salma Tawfik // Lucas Mei
