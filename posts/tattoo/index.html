Tattoo

Char O'Brien

2024/03/14

Four words. Anytime I look down to my arm, I am reminded of four words

I love myself today.

They stare back at me in deep black. The letters grow closer together with every passing day. They are a little crooked. They are not perfect. The s is a little thicker than the y. There is a freckle above the l.

These words pierced me. Scratching my veins. It hurt and it made me bleed. But a cool cloth wiped away the blood and let me read my body.

I love myself today.

The funny thing is, I didn’t love myself. I couldn’t find myself in the mirror. My mouth could not keep up with my brain. My feet could not keep up with my legs. The body I was in did not feel like mine.

But when I draw on my skin, it becomes my own. I am my body. How can I have this body if no one else will have the same one? It must share a home with me. These four words remind me of who I am. They are not perfect and neither am I.

No one else can look at their arm and see these four words like I do.

I love myself today.
